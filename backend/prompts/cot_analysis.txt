## GRANTOR AND GRANTEE EXTRACTION RULES

Extract Grantor and Grantee **name recitals** verbatim from each instrument. "Verbatim" applies to names, titles, capacities, and entity designations — not to addresses or other non-name information listed under "Omit" below.

**Include:**
- All "also known as" (a/k/a) and "formerly known as" (fka) recitals.
- The full name of any Trust recited, including the date of the Trust.
- All fiduciary recitals: Administrator, Administratrix, Executor, Executrix, Personal Representative of the Estate of (include identification of the estate recital).
- All recitals to tenancy, which may be located after the Witness clause (Testimonium Clause) or in a specific Tenancy of Co-Ownership clause.
- All recitals as to marital status and recitals to sole or separate property.
- For non-natural persons such as Corporations, include the recital to the state and entity type (e.g., "a Colorado corporation").
- Any recitals to an interest in the land conveyed under 100% — include in the Comments (this is typically identified as "as to an undivided interest" followed by a fraction or percentage).
- Specific party recitals such as "Owner(s) of premises described in Exhibit A."
- Limiting recitals such as "John Doe, exclusively."

**Special Cases:**
- In the event of a tenancy recital that begins with "not as" followed by "but as," use only the portion of the recital that is in the affirmative. Note: sometimes the affirmative precedes the negative.
- In the event a Trust is recited without recital to a Trustee and "Trustee" does not appear in the signature block, extract the name of the Trust verbatim.

**Omit:**
- Any address information for the Grantor or Grantee.
- Any recital to domicile of a natural person (e.g., "John Doe, of Springfield, CO").
- Any generic party recital such as "Property Owners."
- Any recital such as "heirs and assigns forever."

## LEGAL/COMMENTS COLUMN RULES

**Include:**
- For a deed of trust or mortgage, include the loan amount or principal amount recited.
- If a marginal reference appears on an instrument (e.g., "Partial Payoff of Contract…" in the upper left-hand corner of a page), include it.
- Include recitals to gift deeds or gift conveyances.
- Include any specific recital such as "subject to easement in Reception No. 12345."

**Omit:**
- Any recital to consideration or amount of consideration.
- In the event the instrument is "Subject Premises and more," omit including the description for additional property.
- If the Document Caption column is self-explanatory as to the nature of the instrument, omit repetitive language in the Legal/Comments column. For example, if the Document Caption is "Certificate of Authority for the XYZ Trust," there is no need to include a descriptor such as "Trust administration document" — however, affirmatively state the result of the instrument (in this instance, identifying verbatim the Trustees, Co-Trustees, etc., as recited).
- Any generic recital such as "Subject to easements," "Subject to taxes," etc.

**Top Margin Annotations:**
1. If **"Reference"** appears at the center top margin of the first page of an instrument, the only comment required is "Reference."
2. If **"Mineral reservation"** appears at the center top margin of the first page of an instrument, the only comment required is "Mineral reservation."
3. If **"Covenant(s)"** appears at the center top margin of the first page of an instrument, the comment is to be based solely on the highlighted portion of the instrument.

## STRUCK / NON-RELEVANT PORTIONS OF PAGES

- Where the user has struck a part of a page, disregard the struck portion of the page.
- At times, the user will not strike the non-relevant portion of the page. In such an instance, note in the Comments that it appears that a portion of the page of an instrument has been included but is likely not an instrument that affects the Subject Tract.
  - This is especially evident in instruments prior to the 1950s, which are a transcript of the instrument rather than a copy of the actual instrument — a scrivener hand wrote or typed a transcript without regard to separation by page.

- If an unpatented Mining Claim Location is included in the COT, include it in chronological order (as determined by user input) as a Reference at the end of the COT.
- In the event of a Patented Mining Claim, include it in the COT as any other surface interest.
- For a Patent from the United States of America, of particular note is the reservation clause in the paragraph before the signature block of the President of the United States of America. Include in the Comments the clause beginning "subject to…"

## RESERVATIONS

- Pay close attention to any language of reservation in instruments in the COT (e.g., "Grantor's reserve…").
- Include all such reservations in the Comments.

## PERSONAL PROPERTY

- Do not include any references to personal property in the Comments (e.g., "household furniture," livestock, or other personal effects).

## HEIR AND ESTATE INTERESTS

- Pay particular attention to and include in the Comments:
  - Interests of heirs and their status as a community interest.
  - Their relation to a decedent or status as a minor.
  - Stated fractional interests (e.g., an undivided one-half interest).

---

## CONDEMNATION — JUDGMENT ON DECLARATION OF TAKING

- A Judgment on Declaration of Taking is a condemnation of an easement on multiple properties and is very common in linear projects.
- When condemned parcels are described by Parcel Number:
  - Include in the Comments the Parcel Number of the Subject Premises.
  - Enumerate all rights taken, including: the easement itself, appurtenances such as rights to adjacent lands, rights of entry, and broader rights such as operation and maintenance.
  - Pay particular attention to whether appurtenant rights exist outside of the boundaries of the described easement condemned.

---

## PROBATE ORDERS AND DECREES

- For probate orders/decrees, be certain to identify:
  - The Estate of the deceased.
  - The fiduciary appointed — who may be an Executor/Executrix, Administrator/Administratrix, Personal Representative, Trustee, or other fiduciary title.
  - In an intestate administration: the surviving heirs-at-law and their percent interests as ordered.
- Note in the Comments whether the real property recited is the Subject Premises or Subject Premises as more particularly described.
- Omit comments on personal property from the Comments.

---

## MISSING INSTRUMENTS

- At times a copy of an instrument is missing in the COT.
- Analyze the abstract of information from the missing instrument the same as you would analyze an actual copy of the instrument.

---

## MINERAL INTERESTS

- Comment on mineral interests when they appear in the instruments reviewed.
- Some form templates may instruct you to ignore mineral comments; others may require full comments on mineral interests (e.g., a Quitclaim Deed conveying mineral interests). Follow the form template instructions if provided.

---

## JOINT TENANCY AND TENANCY RECITALS

- Omit references to Acceptance of Joint Tenancy that may be included at the end of an instrument, where the tenancy recital is unambiguous (e.g., "…as joint tenants with right of survivorship,…").
- If the title of an instrument is Joint Tenancy Deed, the tenancy of the Grantees is joint tenants with right of survivorship without further recital required.

---

## CORRECTIONS TO INSTRUMENTS

- In the event of a correction to an instrument:
  - Note in the Comments whether the change was initialed by the parties.
  - If not initialed, note that — this is not a fatal flaw, but a notable observation.

---

## AFFIDAVITS

- **Statutory affidavits** required by State or local law (e.g., Affidavit of Disclosure) are not required to be reported in the COT, nor in the Comments.
- **Evidentiary affidavits** (e.g., Proof of Death & Heirship, Private Burial Affidavit) should be reported in the COT and analyzed like any other instrument.
- For all affidavits that are reported:
  - Omit any reference to cause of death.
  - Omit any reference to a filing party.

---

## EASEMENTS

- Be certain to note easements created by an instrument in the Comments (e.g., an easement for wellsite access and utilities).
- Affirmatively state if an easement has any exclusivity recital.
- In such instances, include the easement created in the Comments and prompt the user to generate an exhibit for the easement.

---

## STATE AND GOVERNMENT LIENS

- Pay particular attention to liens that are in favor of the State or an arm of the State (e.g., a State health care lien), as these may have no expiration date.
- If state-specific lien duration data is provided in the form template, apply it. Otherwise, note the lien and flag for user review.

---

## LEGAL DOCTRINES

- **Doctrine of After Acquired Title:** In a chronological chain of title, it is possible for interests to be conveyed at a later time, where an outstanding interest is cured by a later recorded conveyance. Recognize and account for this.
- **Doctrine of Merger:** When the same entity holds both an easement interest and fee title interest in the same property, note in the Legal/Comments column that the easement and fee interests have merged. For example, if a pipeline company "ABC, Inc." has an earlier recorded easement that affects a parcel of land and that same entity later acquires fee title interest to the property, note the merger.
